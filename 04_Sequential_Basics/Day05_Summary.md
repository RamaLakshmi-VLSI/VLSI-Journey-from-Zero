# Day 5: Finite State Machines (FSM)

## Concepts Covered:
* **Mealy vs. Moore Models:** Analyzed the trade-off between Moore (stable, state-dependent output) and Mealy (fast, input-dependent output).
* **State Diagrams:** Designed a 101 Sequence Detector (Overlapping) using state transition bubbles and arrows.
* **State Encoding:** Evaluated Binary, One-Hot, and Gray encoding schemes.
* **Hardware Trade-offs:** Discussed the Area vs. Speed (Frequency) trade-offs when choosing between Binary and One-Hot encoding for VLSI.

## Takeaway:
An FSM is the "brain" of digital logic. Mastering state transitions is the precursor to writing RTL (Verilog). In a professional environment, choosing the right encoding (like One-Hot for high-speed FPGAs) is what differentiates a design engineer from a student.
