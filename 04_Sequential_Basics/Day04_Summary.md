# Day 4: Sequential Logic Fundamentals

## Concepts Covered:
* **Storage Elements:** Mastered the transition from SR Latches (basic feedback) to D-Latches (level-triggered) and D Flip-Flops (edge-triggered).
* **Clocking Architectures:** Analyzed the Master-Slave D-Flip-Flop to understand how data is captured on a clock edge.
* **T & JK Flip-Flops:** Implemented toggle logic and understood the universal nature of JK logic in avoiding invalid states.
* **Registers:** Explored SISO, SIPO, PISO, and PIPO configurations for serial-to-parallel data conversion.
* **Counters:** Compared Synchronous vs. Asynchronous (Ripple) counters, focusing on the propagation delay trade-offs essential for high-speed design.

## Takeaway:
Sequential logic is the backbone of architectural control. Mastery of Setup and Hold times in Flip-Flops is critical for ensuring timing closure, while understanding the "ripple" effect in counters is the first step toward managing clock skew in a professional VLSI environment.
