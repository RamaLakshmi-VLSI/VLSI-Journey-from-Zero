# Day 2: Logic Optimization

## Concepts Covered:
* **Boolean Laws:** Commutative, Associative, Distributive, De-Morgan’s, and Consensus Law.
* **K-Maps:** Learned 3-variable and 4-variable mapping.
* **Minimization:** Mastered the "Wrap-around" or "Rolling" grouping technique.

## Practice Problem:
* Function: F(A, B, C) = Σm(0, 2, 4, 6)
* Simplified Expression: [C'] (C-Bar)

### MUX as a Universal Gate
* Successfully implemented AND, OR, and XOR using a 2:1 MUX.
* Mastered the "Block Observation" method for MUX inputs.
* XOR Implementation: S=A, I0=B, I1=B'.

## Takeaway:
Mastering K-Map reduction and Boolean laws is the first step toward area-efficient VLSI design. Minimizing gate count directly reduces the physical "footprint" and power consumption on a silicon chip.
